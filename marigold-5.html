The Marigold-5 is a compact DC-DC power module engineered for the FIRST Robotics Competition. It provides four high-current USB-C ports to power co-processors, and other peripherals directly from the robot's 12V supply.

WHY MARIGOLD-5?

Brownout-Proof Power: FRC batteries sag from 12.5V down to 7V during pushing matches. Most common adapters cut out around 10V, rebooting your coprocessor exactly when you need it most. Marigold-5 operates reliably down to 7V, keeping connected devices stable throughout the match.

Built for Hungry Devices: Vision systems require significant processing power. Power dual co-processors for redundant vision, or run your Raspberry Pi, network switch, and LED ring from a single module. 10A total output means you don't have to choose between devices.

Quick Replacement: Standard #10-32 mounting holes and ring-terminal power connections mean you can swap a failed unit in minutes. Perfect for when you're between qualification matches. No soldering, no custom brackets, just a few bolts and your back in the game.

Designed for FRC: We've lost matches to power failures. We've watched co-processors reboot during autonomous. We've seen reversed polarity connections destroy boards in the pit. Marigold-5 is designed to address the real problems teams face at competition.
